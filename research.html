Research

Exploring the frontiers of physics, computation, and complex systems.

My research interests lie at the interface of Quantum Physics, Computational Science, and Complex Systems. I am particularly fascinated by quantum information, condensed matter phenomena, and the use of high-performance computing to simulate and analyze physical systems.

Quantum Physics & Quantum Computing: Studying the fundamentals of quantum mechanics and quantum information. Interested in quantum algorithms, entanglement, and the simulation of quantum systems using both classical and quantum computers.
Condensed Matter Physics: Exploring the collective behavior of many-body systems, including phase transitions, superconductivity, and emergent phenomena in low-dimensional systems.
Computational Physics: Developing and applying computational methods to solve complex problems in physics, such as Monte Carlo simulations, numerical solutions of differential equations, and data analysis with Python and C++.
Nonlinear Dynamics & Stochastic Processes: Investigating systems governed by nonlinear interactions and randomness, including chaos theory, pattern formation, and stochastic modeling in natural sciences.
