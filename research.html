Research

Exploring the frontiers of astrophysics, cosmology, and the interstellar medium.

My research interests lie at the intersection of astrophysics and cosmology, particularly in the role of the interstellar and circumgalactic media in galaxy evolution, the physics of star formation, and gravitational wave astrophysics. I also explore connections to dark matter, continuous gravitational wave signals from neutron stars, and the early universe.

Gravitational Wave Astronomy Exploring the nature of spacetime, neutron stars, and dark sectors through continuous and stochastic gravitational wave signals. Particularly interested in how gravitational waves can probe the Big Bang and compact binary systems.
Circumgalactic and Interstellar Media Investigating gas flows in and around galaxies, and their roles in fueling star formation. Current work involves theoretical modeling of CGM gas properties and understanding ISM structures during galaxy evolution.
Star Formation and Galaxy Evolution Studying the observational and theoretical aspects of star formation, including collapse processes, feedback, and the regulation of star formation rates across cosmic time.
Early Universe & Reionization Understanding the Epoch of Reionization, how the first stars and galaxies ionized the intergalactic medium, and the observational strategies (like 21-cm cosmology) to study this crucial era.
